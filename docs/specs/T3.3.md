# T3.3 Legacy API Mapper Specification

Status: in progress

## Contract

The mapper consumes positioned T3.2 legacy ability AST nodes and either emits
a typed Forge `Expression` plus typed costs or returns a stable quarantine
reason. It never substitutes prose or an approximate effect for an unsupported
legacy value.

Mapper registration is table-driven by the pair `(line prefix, legacy API)`.
Each registered mapper owns its accepted selector keys, parameter keys, value
grammar, target lowering, cost lowering, and structural test pack. Unknown
APIs, unknown parameters, duplicate parameters, missing required parameters,
dynamic values without an exact Forge representation, and unsupported target
languages all fail closed.

`metrics/api_coverage.json` reports each API's `legacy_uses`, `mapped`,
`verified`, and `quarantined` counts in descending corpus-frequency order.
`verified` currently means that the accepted structural mapping family has a
dedicated mapper test; it does not mean card-specific behavior has passed T3.6.
`metrics/api_quarantine.json` retains reason counts and deterministic samples.

## Initial Mapping Pack

The first pack targets exact high-frequency primitive cases:

- simple `A:... Mana` abilities with basic mana/tap costs and closed produced
  mana forms;
- simple `A:... Draw` abilities for the activating player with static positive
  card counts; and
- simple `A:... DealDamage` abilities with a positive literal amount and
  `Any` target language.

Complex costs, chained sub-abilities, dynamic amounts, replacement riders,
conditional execution, and narrower target predicates remain quarantined until
their value lowerers land. The initial pinned-corpus result maps 1,552 of
43,649 top-level ability uses (3.5556%) and quarantines the rest without
approximation.

The second pack adds exact static-integer Pump/PumpAll, life change, mill,
tap/untap, destroy, counter placement, and battlefield Continuous mappings.
Continuous mappings cover simple power/toughness modifiers and closed keyword
grants over self, aura/equipment, controller/owner, other-object, card-type,
and subtype selectors. Dynamic values, conditions, linked abilities, and
off-battlefield effects remain quarantined. The pinned-corpus result after this
pack maps 3,406 of 43,649 top-level ability uses (7.8032%).

The next packs continue in measured impact order, beginning with event APIs
whose Execute/SubAbility references require explicit SVar graph resolution.

The third pack adds script-level SVar graph resolution with stable diagnostics
for missing, duplicate, cyclic, malformed, and unsupported links. Exact ETB,
upkeep, attack, and spell-cast triggers can now resolve cost-free supported
effect chains. It also adds simple battlefield zone moves, token creation,
mass destroy/damage, discard, counterspell, scry, and surveil mappings. The
pinned-corpus result after this pack maps 6,102 of 43,649 top-level ability
uses (13.9797%) without approximating unsupported graph nodes.

The fourth pack records quarantine reasons per API and adds exact generic cost
reducers, simple damage triggers, fully resolved modal Charm graphs,
unconditional unblockability statics, and simple battlefield bulk zone moves.
The pinned-corpus result after this pack maps 6,486 of 43,649 top-level ability
uses (14.8594%).

The fifth pack expands exact battlefield target selectors, temporary keyword
grants, static type/color/PT/control changes, and moved-event replacement
graphs. The sixth pack adds structured costs for self-sacrifice, life payment,
loyalty changes, one-counter removal, self-exile, and discard while continuing
to quarantine unsupported selector costs. Nonsemantic legacy presentation and
AI fields are classified centrally. The pinned-corpus result after these packs
maps 9,114 of 43,649 top-level ability uses (20.8802%).

The seventh pack adds simple Animate effects with static base PT, additive
types, overwrite colors, closed keyword grants, optional ability removal, and
permanent or end-of-turn duration. The pinned-corpus result after this pack
maps 9,205 of 43,649 top-level ability uses (21.0887%).

The eighth pack adds exact draw, attack-declaration, and block event families;
closed opponent, ownership, type, subtype, color, keyword, power, toughness,
and mana-value selectors; filtered counterspells; unconditional alternative
costs; identity-bound zone moves; and structured sacrifice, discard, exile,
zero-loyalty, and multi-counter-removal costs. Conditional and aggregate
events, dynamic values, combat-state selectors, remembered bindings, library
ordering, and unsupported cost kinds remain quarantined. The pinned-corpus
result after this pack maps 11,024 of 43,649 top-level ability uses (25.2560%)
and quarantines the remaining 32,625 with stable reason codes.

The ninth pack adds exact primitive mappings for sacrifice, control change,
damage prevention, bulk counter changes, spell copying, extra turns,
untap-all, tap-or-untap choices, proliferate, and unconditional attack/block/
cast restrictions. It also adds optional and secondary trigger lowering,
target/discard/counter-added events, explicit battlefield/colorless selector
predicates, and closed origin-to-destination zone-change events. The
pinned-corpus result after this pack maps 12,347 of 43,649 top-level ability
uses (28.2870%) and quarantines the remaining 31,302 with stable reason codes.

The tenth pack adds closed player targeting, once-per-damage markers, owner
state effects, investigate, attachment, shuffle and life-setting effects,
whole-group tap, keyword removal, mass animation, hand reveal/look, transform,
combined attack/block restrictions, blocked and unblocked combat events, and
cast-or-copy event markers. Unsupported conditions, dynamic values, timing
restrictions, remembered bindings, and event shapes that exceed the frozen
operation surface continue to fail closed. The pinned-corpus result after this
pack maps 13,143 of 43,649 top-level ability uses (30.1107%) and quarantines
the remaining 30,506 with stable reason codes.

The current expansion also normalizes exact legacy defaults, lowers sorcery,
your-turn, and once-per-turn activation timing, preserves the destroy
without-regeneration marker, accepts exact Defined damage selectors, expands
fixed token-script bundles, and lowers closed library searches plus targeted
and bulk nonbattlefield zone moves. PC-0005's first mapper wave adds closed
presence/count conditions and exact dynamic values for paid X, selectors,
counters, devotion, distinct colors, target characteristics, and turn history.
The pinned-corpus result maps 15,506 of 43,649 top-level ability uses (35.5243%)
and quarantines the remaining 28,143 with stable reason codes. Priority work is
ranked by requested cards unlocked and linked-ability fan-out, while
corpus-wide coverage remains independently binding.

The bounded PC-0006 batch adds fail-closed handling for `ValidBlocker`,
`Condition`, `ValidTgts`, `CheckSVar`, and `SVarCompare`. Shared condition
lowering composes presence predicates, exact turn-state conditions, and closed
value-SVar comparisons around triggers, replacements, statics, spells, and
activated abilities according to their ability kind. Stateful or
trigger-object-dependent named conditions remain value-quarantined until the
IR has a real binding for them. `CantBlockBy` and per-creature block triggers
retain blocker selectors; the 33 aggregate `AttackerBlocked` cases remain
parameter-quarantined because the current two-argument event cannot also retain
aggregate cardinality. Player-targeted draw, token, marker, and bulk effects
retain target ownership or control scope. Unknown conditions, unresolved value
SVars, unsupported comparison operands, and ambiguous target-owner
combinations remain quarantined. This is structural mapper evidence only;
production execution and card semantics remain owned by T3.5 and T3.6. The
pinned-corpus result after this batch maps 16,020 of 43,649 top-level ability
uses (36.7019%) and emits 9,002 of 33,290 complete scripts (27.0412%), including
177 of 365 Owner-priority identities.

## All-Confirmed-Blocker Planner

PC-0006 adds `forge-porttools legacy blocker-plan`. One parallel corpus pass
evaluates every top-level ability, every reachable SVar ability, and every
keyword independently. For the dominant `UNSUPPORTED_PARAMETER` class, the
planner removes each diagnosed unknown parameter from a temporary copy and
re-evaluates the node, exposing all currently confirmable parameter gaps in one
pass. It then retains the first remaining non-parameter failure. Unreferenced
SVars do not create noise; referenced sub-abilities carry their root fan-out.

The planner writes a compact summary to `metrics/blocker_plan.json` and
per-card detail under `target/t3-blocker-plan/`. Families are normalized,
deduplicated per card, weighted by T0/T1/T2 Owner priority, and ranked with
linked-root fan-out plus an explicit effort prior that orders parameter, value,
keyword, and new-API work from cheapest to most expensive. A deterministic
greedy pass recommends six batches of five families by effort-normalized
marginal cards whose complete confirmed blocker set would be addressed. These
are planning estimates only: actual coverage changes only when the normal
translator emits and compiler-roundtrips a card.

The current local pass analyzed all 33,290 scripts, found confirmed blockers in
24,163 scripts including 182 priority scripts, and recorded 61,756 observations
across 5,033 normalized families with linked-root fan-out 62,777. Forty-three
porttools tests cover independent roots, reachable and unused SVars, keyword
gaps, parameter peeling, normalization, deterministic batching, and repeatable
full-corpus report generation, plus condition, blocker, and target-player
lowering.
