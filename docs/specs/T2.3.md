# T2.3 Replacement And Prevention Effects

T2.3 adds a declarative CR 614/615 interception substrate for damage events.
Like T2.2 triggers, replacement definitions are inert data compiled from card
IR later; no closures or card-specific code run inside the kernel.

## Public Surface

- `ReplacementEffectId`: stable handle for one registered effect.
- `ReplacementDefinition`: data-only controller/source/condition/operation
  bundle.
- `ReplacementCondition::DamageWouldBeDealt`: matches source, target, and
  combat-only damage predicates.
- `ReplacementOperation`: supports prevent all, prevent N, add N, double, and
  set damage.
- `ReplacementDuration`: persistent or once-only.
- `Action::RegisterReplacementEffect`: installs one replacement/prevention
  definition.
- `Action::SetReplacementChoiceOrder`: records the affected player's
  deterministic ordering preference for simultaneous applicable effects.

## Interception

Damage events pass through the replacement pipeline before final mutation.
The pipeline applies each matching effect at most once to the event, recomputes
the damage amount after each application, retires once-only effects after they
apply, and emits `ReplacementEffectApplied` audit events. Damage reduced from a
positive amount to zero does not emit final `DamageMarked` or
`CombatDamageDealt` events.

Combat damage records store final post-replacement damage. Lifelink,
deathtouch, lethal damage, and state-based actions therefore use the modified
amount, not the originally assigned amount.

## Ordering

Self-replacement effects are ordered ahead of normal effects, regardless of the
affected player's stored preference. Normal effects are ordered by the affected
player's `SetReplacementChoiceOrder` preference, then by deterministic effect ID
for omitted effects. For player damage, the affected player chooses. For object
damage, the object's controller chooses.

## Determinism

Registered effects, choice-order preferences, lifecycle events, and application
events participate in canonical bytes and streaming hashes. The clone-surface
baseline now accounts for replacement definition and choice-order vectors.

## Coverage

Unit tests cover affected-player ordering, self-replacement precedence,
prevention of combat consequences, once-only retirement, and hash consistency.
Oracle scenarios `t2_3_replacement_order_damage.ron` and
`t2_3_prevent_all_combat_damage.ron` cover damage prevention vs replacement
stacking through the public scenario runner.
